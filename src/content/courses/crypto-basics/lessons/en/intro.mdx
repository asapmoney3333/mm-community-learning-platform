# What is Crypto?

Crypto is a form of **digital money** that moves across internet-based networks instead of through paper cash or a single bank database.

<Callout title="Big Idea" tone="info">
  Crypto combines software, shared record-keeping, and internet access so people can send value without relying on one central company to approve every step.
</Callout>

## Why people care

- It can be sent across borders quickly.
- It gives users direct control over their wallets.
- It introduces public, shared transaction records on blockchains.

## Core terms

<Glossary>
  <GlossaryItem term="Blockchain">
    A shared record of transactions that is updated in ordered blocks.
  </GlossaryItem>
  <GlossaryItem term="Wallet">
    The app or device that helps you manage your crypto addresses and keys.
  </GlossaryItem>
  <GlossaryItem term="Private key">
    A secret credential that proves control over funds. It should never be shared.
  </GlossaryItem>
</Glossary>

<Callout title="Safety first" tone="warning">
  If someone gets your private key or recovery phrase, they can usually control your funds. Convenience matters, but security matters more.
</Callout>

## One simple mental model

Think of crypto as a new kind of financial rail:

1. A wallet creates and signs a transaction.
2. The network checks whether it is valid.
3. The blockchain records the result for others to verify.

That does not make every crypto project trustworthy, but it explains why the technology feels different from a normal payment app.
